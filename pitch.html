MLB Pitches
Exploratory Analysis

About

Thanks to the Moneyball era of the Oakland Athletics in 2002-2003, decision making in modern day baseball has become extremely data-driven. Nearly every single statistic is now recorded ranging from pitch velocity to bat speed to even the time it takes for a team to warm up in between innings. As a college baseball player who was constantly thrown statistics every time I reunited with the team for practices and games, I was captivated by the intricate measures and insights you can pull from the game of baseball.

Contrast to professional baseball teams, college baseball programs don’t have access to data teams to allow coaches and players to make data-driven decisions. College baseball teams also don’t have as much recorded data as the MLB such as pitch and swing metrics. Therefore, I had a value to add to the offense of my baseball team and give them insight to pitches they may encounter when at-bat. Since there wasn’t data on pitches across college baseball, I used data from Major League Baseball because the game is very similar from college to professional. With this data, I performed an exploratory data analysis to give more insight to pitches for the offense on my team to have a better sense of what’s coming at them.
