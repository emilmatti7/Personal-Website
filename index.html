WHO IS EMIL?

I was born and raised in a tight knit community in Parsippany, New Jersey located 40 minutes outside of New York City. I’m proud to say that I am a Filipino-American and raised by two immigrant parents. Growing up with an older brother who is 7 years older brought the competitive nature out of me. Countless losses to my brother in wiffle ball, monopoly and video games has only shaped me into someone who isn’t afraid to fail and chase what I want. My big brother set the bar high for me growing up and I’m forever grateful for that.

Sports have been a huge part of my life, however, baseball was something that I had a passion for and dedicated my life to. Waking up at 6 am for workouts before school and spending my summers playing 60 games in 90 degree weather has brought me many lessons beyond the game itself. My love for the game allowed me to meet so many people, see many different places and earn a scholarship to play at a Division 1 school. The path to playing in college was no easy feat but with enough love in what you do and the right people around you makes everything worthwhile.

College opened my eyes to so many different things. I attended Wagner College in New York City where I studied Economics while juggling a full baseball schedule including team weight lifts, practices and games. There were a few takeaways I got from my experience at college. The first being that personal connection creates a better atmosphere and conveys value and appreciation in a team setting. I made it my mission to meet as many people as I can since personal relationships are something I value the most. Secondly, in college you will be put into a bunch of different groups and it is a great time to practice your leadership skills. It is important to get out of your comfort zone and put yourself in groups/situations you wouldn’t normally be in as I believe it is the only way to grow. The last and biggest takeaway I received from college is patience. You meet so many people with different values and upbringings and learning to be patient when working with others is a skill I always strive to progress in. College helped me grasp this concept as it is a skill that is so important in life since, whether you like it or not, you will constantly be surrounded with all different types of people.

What I’ve learned through my childhood, baseball and college is valuable and applicable to my life in every aspect. We all have a choice to either sleepwalk through life or make the most of every moment. What will you choose?
